Iteratively Designing Electry

Electry is pitched as “LinkedIn for skilled blue-collar workers.” and is a part of Y'20's cohort
In a group project, we designed Electry's web interface, made a hi-fi mockup and user-tested it. We chose Electry, because we liked the idea of exploring a familiar problem — finding employment — in a different industry — blue-collar work.

  • Type: UI/UX Project

  • Timeline: November 2020

1. Hi-Fi Mockup

Essentially, Electry is a job board to connect blue-collar workers with companies looking to hire them. Companies publish job openings on the platform, workers to find opportunities that match their qualifications and preferences, then the companies can follow-up with applicants off-platform.

Notable Design Choices:

  • Focus on employees and improve their job seeking experience
  • Emphasizes essential information about each job through a succint profile building process
  • Provides personalized experience through onboarding, exploring job openings, and expressing interest

2. User Testing

Task

Scenario: You are a trained blue-collar worker looking for a job.

  • Subtask 1: Sign up by completing the onboarding process. Be sure to answer every question in the survey.
  • Subtask 2: Now that you have gotten a list of employers that match your preferences, explore your recommended jobs and share your profile with one employer.

Post-Test Questions

  1. Were you successful at completing the task? How difficult was it?
  2. What frustrated you most about this site?
  3. If you had a magic wand, how would you improve this site?
  4. What did you like about the site?

Analysis of Results

Based on the Tasks + Subtasks, the User Testing results show that users were generally able to complete the tasks without any issues. The results reveal that users enjoyed how the interface was simple to use, concise and effectively indicated progress with visual cues.

Future Considerations

  • On the “My Jobs” page, add a go back button to navigate back to recommended jobs
  • When editing profiling, allow users to click between profile sections

Final Thoughts

This project helped me understand the value of user testing. I was pleasantly surprised by the positive response and success in user completing tasks. Having a user's perspective opened my mind to limitations and opportunities for improvement that I would not have found otherwise.
